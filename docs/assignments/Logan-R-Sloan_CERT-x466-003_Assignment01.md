# **Essay**

* [Wikipedia Graph Data Story Example](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JheGL6uSF-4)

	The data story is to tell the creator’s exploration through Wikipedia and how, using algorithmic clustering, the articles relate to each other and the separate “communities” and edge-cases therein. The data used is a scrape of every article in the English Wikipedia database. The creator began the deep-dive because of his interest in what is known as the “Wikipedia Race”, which is a challenge to get from one article of Wikipedia to another clicking through the shortest number of links. Some of the main takeaways are that not all articles are linked together, some are orphans (articles without in-coming links), some dead-ends (articles without any out-going links), and some are even dead-end orphans (articles without any in-coming or out-going links). Another major takeaway is that most articles on the English Wikipedia can be linked from another article within ten or fewer links; however, because of the edge-cases just mentioned not all articles can be reached. The longest path the creator found was 61-links as the shortest path to one article from another—although longer shortest-paths may exist (the amount of processing power for this kind of path finding is extremely high and time consuming). I think the author did an excellent job with the exploration and presentation, especially in regards to making such a niche subject very appealing, entertaining, and understandable. At this moment, I am not sure there is much I would change. (The video is linked at the top of this essay.)
